Frequently Asked Questions (FAQ)

What is the purpose of the Mars Rover program?

The Mars Rover program is designed to explore the surface of Mars, study its geology, climate, and search for signs of past or present life.

How many Mars Rovers has NASA sent?

NASA has sent five rovers to Mars: Sojourner, Spirit, Opportunity, Curiosity, and Perseverance.

How do Mars Rovers communicate with Earth?

Rovers use antennas to send signals to Mars orbiters, which relay the data back to Earth. Direct communication is also possible using the Deep Space Network.

How are Mars Rovers powered?

Early rovers like Sojourner, Spirit, and Opportunity were powered by solar panels. Newer rovers like Curiosity and Perseverance use nuclear power (MMRTG - Multi-Mission Radioisotope Thermoelectric Generator).

How long do Mars Rovers last?

The operational life of Mars Rovers varies. For example, Spirit was designed to last 90 days but operated for nearly six years. Curiosity has been active for over a decade.

Can Mars Rovers return samples to Earth?

Not yet. However, Perseverance is designed to collect and store samples that a future mission will bring back to Earth.
